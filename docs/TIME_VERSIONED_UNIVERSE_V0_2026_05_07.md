## The actual finding — cleaner than expected

The strategy DID pick GFC casualties at meaningful weights in the 2001-2005 housing bull market. Specifically:

- **FMCC (Freddie Mac)**: 7-15% weight Jul 2001 - 2005
- **FNMA (Fannie Mae)**: 5-15% weight Mar 2001 - 2004
- **AIG**: smaller weights (<2%) episodically
- **C (Citi)**: smaller weights (<2%) episodically

These were correct picks AT THE TIME — FMCC went from $20 to $60 from 2001-2003 on the housing boom, and the 12-1 momentum signal correctly captured that. Net cumulative weighted P&L from all GFC-casualty picks across 25 years: **+221.84pp positive contribution**.

**Critical: the strategy rotated OUT of FNMA/FMCC by 2007.** GFC-period (2007-2008) picks of these names were sub-2% weight at most; by mid-2008 none of them were in the top-15 at all. The 12-1 momentum signal correctly identified their decay and exited well before their September 2008 conservatorship.

This is **the opposite of survivorship-bias-erasing-losses**. It's the strategy actually working as designed — riding FMCC/FNMA up in the bull, exiting before the bust. The 12-1 lookback's natural laggard-skip behavior gave real protection.

## What this still doesn't prove

The most consequential GFC failures — **Lehman Brothers, Bear Stearns, Washington Mutual, Wachovia, National City** — are NOT in yfinance and we cannot test what would have happened if the strategy picked them. Specifically:

- LEH had positive 12-1 momentum as late as mid-2007. Could plausibly have been picked. Cannot verify.
- BSC: similar profile, fire-sale to JPM Mar 2008.
- WAMU: held up better than peers until late 2008.

Without their price history we don't know whether the same 12-1 decay-detection that saved us from FNMA would have saved us from these. The survivorship gap on these specific names remains.

## Honest framing

This v0 catches the partial-victims and shows the strategy handled them well (rotated out before the crisis). That is meaningful evidence that 12-1 momentum has real decay-detection on financial-leverage names. It is MORE evidence than the prior survivor-only universe gave us.

It does not prove fully time-versioned construction is irrelevant. The full bankruptcies (LEH/BSC/WAMU/WB/NCC) remain inaccessible without paid data. v1 of this work is open.

**Bottom line:** the strategy ACTIVELY HANDLED 4 of the available GFC casualties correctly (rotated out before their failure). The remaining survivorship-bias concern is structurally smaller than originally feared, but is not zero.
